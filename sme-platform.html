SME Platform 101

This page explains how SME IPOs work in India, how they differ from mainboard IPOs, and why I use a Beyond Financials approach to evaluate risk and quality.

SME IPO vs Mainboard IPO

Factor SME Platform Mainboard
Issue SizeSmaller issues, early growth companiesLarger issues, mature and scaled issuers
LiquidityLower, can be volatile and thinRelatively deeper liquidity
VolatilityHigh, frequent sharp movesGenerally lower than SME
Minimum ApplicationOften higher lot size for retailLower lot size in many cases
Evaluation NeedRequires deep qualitative filteringFundamentals often carry more weight

How SME IPOs work

  • Company files DRHP/RHP and appoints lead managers.
  • Price discovery depends on issue structure (book-built or fixed price).
  • Allocation dynamics involve retail, HNI, and often anchor participation.
  • Market maker structure can influence post-listing liquidity behavior.
  • Post-listing momentum and sustainability can diverge quickly.

Risk and reward profile

  • Potential for exceptional wealth creation if quality and timing align.
  • Severe drawdowns possible if valuation, governance, or execution fails.
  • Information asymmetry and narrative risk are higher than mainboard.
  • Position sizing and discipline are critical in this segment.

My personal lens: Why Beyond Financials matters

In this segment, financials are necessary but not sufficient. I have seen companies list on strong narratives and decent numbers, only to underperform in later quarters due to margin compression, unrealistic growth projections, or valuation excesses. My framework combines financial understanding with lead manager quality, anchor behavior, issue context, timing patterns, and market structure signals.
