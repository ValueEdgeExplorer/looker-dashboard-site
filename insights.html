Insights

Short, actionable observations derived from multi-factor dashboard exploration and ongoing market tracking.

1) Lead Manager Consistency and Outcome Quality

Certain lead managers repeatedly bring better-priced, better-executed issuers. A manager-level filter can materially improve hit rates.

Explore in Dashboard

2) Seasonal Clustering Effects

When too many IPOs bunch into similar windows, post-listing performance often weakens. Timing relative to earnings seasons also matters.

Explore in Dashboard

3) Anchor + Lead Manager Combination Signals

Single-factor interpretation can be noisy. Anchor participation becomes much more informative when read jointly with lead manager track record.

Explore in Dashboard

4) Valuation Excess and Post-Listing Mean Reversion

Some IPOs launch at aggressive valuations relative to pre-IPO rounds and fundamentals, reducing post-listing upside and increasing correction probability.

Explore in Dashboard

5) Market Maker Context Is Under-Analyzed

Market maker patterns can provide useful confidence cues in SME offerings, especially when interpreted with allocation and issue structure.

Explore in Dashboard

6) Back-Testing 2022 Signals Forward

Patterns observed in 2022 listing gain and follow-through behavior show repeatable directional signals when discipline is maintained.
